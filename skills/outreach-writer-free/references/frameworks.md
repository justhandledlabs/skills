# Outreach Frameworks

Use this reference when selecting or applying PAS, MEDDIC, or Challenger for Outreach Writer.

## PAS

PAS means Problem, Agitate, Solution.

Best fit:

- Clear pain or inefficiency
- Operational friction
- Manual work
- Missed revenue
- Avoidable risk
- A direct offer with an obvious before/after

Email shape:

1. Mention the personalized detail.
2. Name a likely problem tied to the prospect's role.
3. Briefly intensify the cost of leaving it alone without exaggeration.
4. Position the offer as a simple next step.
5. Ask for a short call or permission to send a relevant idea.

Avoid:

- Overly negative language
- Fear-based claims
- Unsupported quantified impact

## MEDDIC

MEDDIC means Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion.

Best fit:

- Enterprise or mid-market sales
- Senior buyers
- Budget ownership
- Complex buying committees
- Measurable business outcomes
- Procurement, implementation, compliance, or operational change

Email shape:

1. Mention the personalized detail.
2. Tie the message to a metric the title likely owns.
3. Connect the pain to decision criteria or business impact.
4. Keep the CTA oriented around whether the priority is active.

Useful metric categories:

- Pipeline velocity
- Sales cycle time
- CAC payback
- Overtime or labor cost
- Conversion rate
- Churn
- Support volume
- Compliance exposure
- Forecast accuracy

Avoid:

- Asking too many discovery questions in the first email
- Sounding like an internal qualification checklist
- Inventing a budget, buying process, or champion

## Challenger

Challenger means lead with insight, reframe the status quo, and connect the reframe to a better action.

Best fit:

- Trigger events
- Market shifts
- Competitive pressure
- New product or segment launch
- A hidden cost the prospect may not be tracking
- A fresh point of view that is useful even if they do not buy

Email shape:

1. Mention the personalized detail.
2. Offer a concise commercial insight or reframe.
3. Explain why the old approach may now be expensive, slow, or risky.
4. Connect the offer to the new way forward.
5. Ask if they want the idea or benchmark.

Avoid:

- Being contrarian for its own sake
- Lecturing the prospect
- Making claims that require external proof not present in the profile

## Auto-Detection Heuristic

Choose Challenger when:

- A trigger event is supplied and it suggests timing.
- The email can lead with a useful market or operational insight.

Choose MEDDIC when:

- The prospect is a senior buyer.
- The offer maps to a measurable business metric.
- The sale is likely complex or cross-functional.

Choose PAS when:

- The pain is direct and practical.
- The user did not provide enough context for a stronger Challenger or MEDDIC angle.

If multiple frameworks fit, prefer the one that makes the first sentence most specific and credible.
